Visualizing the Bertrand's Paradox

The Question

What is the probability that a randomly choosen chord in a circle is longer than the side of the inscribed equilateral triangel?

The Probability?

It depends on how you randomly generate the chords! As described by Bertrand, there are three ways to randomly generate chords;

Method of Random Endpoints

Select two points randomly on the circumference of the circle and connect them to form a chord.
The probability that this chord is longer than the side of the inscribed equilateral triangle is 1/3.

Method of Random Radial Points

Select a radius line randomly in the circle, and then choose a point randomly along this radius. Now construct a chord through this chosen point such that the chord is perpendicular to the radius line initially selected.
The probability that this chord is longer than the side of the inscribed equilateral triangle is 1/2

Method of Random Midpoints

Select a point randomly within the confines of the circle, and then construct a chord such that the selected point becomes the midpoint of the selected chord.
The probability that this chord is longer than the side of the inscribed equilateral triangle is 1/4
